Case 5

"Worsening dyspnea"

A 39 year old male presents to the Emergency Department after a week history of worsening shortness of breath, fevers, and sweats.

He has a chronic cough, but no production of yellow sputum. His albuterol use has increased from a baseline of 0 for the past 2 days to 3 to 4 times daily. In the Emergency Department, his oxygen saturation continued to decline to the point where it was decided he should be admitted.

He states he was last admitted to another hospital 2 months ago and 4 months ago to yet another hospital, with 2 to 3 hospitalizations in the past year. He shakes his head as he states he cannot count the number of prior pneumonias he has had.

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY:

  • Asthma
  • Hypertension

PHYSICAL EXAM:

  • General Appearance: No acute distress
  • Respiratory: decreased breath sounds over right lateral lung field and slightly poor air movement with diminished chest expansion; no wheezing, crackles or rhonchi; speaking full sentences
  • Cardiovascular: regular S1/S2, no murmurs, rubs or gallops

Case Questions:

  1. What is the Reid Classification of bronchiectasis?
  2. What affects of bronchiectasis would you expect to see manifest on pulmonary function testing?
  3. What forms of pulmonary aspergillosis?
