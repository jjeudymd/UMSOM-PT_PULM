Case 2

"Pain and Trouble Breathing S/P Fall"

Later the same evening, you receive a page regarding a 39 year old female who is a transfer from an outside hospital s/p fall onto a railroad track.

The patient reports that she leaned forward to look for the train and subsequently fell onto the track. She was helped by bystanders and taken to a hospital.

She has a past medical history of bipolar disorder and PTSD. You send the patient for imaging and review the imaging with the radiologist.

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION:

  • The patient denies nausea, vomiting, and chest pain.
  • She reports shortness of breath and right upper quadrant abdominal pain.

Case Questions:

  1. Describe three variations of pleural collections
  2. Three hours after initial presentation, a follow up chest xray demonstrated deviation of the mediastinum towards the left. What do you think has happened?
  3. After the previous finding, a chest tube was placed. Subsequent radiology report the next afternoon reports unilateral edema on the side of the previous pneumothorax. How would you explain this finding?
