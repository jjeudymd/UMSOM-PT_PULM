Case 1

"Acute Chest Pain"

You are rotating through the emergency department when you are notified of a patient who presents complaining of chest pain.

The patient is a 46 year old female who was in usual good state of health until she suddenly collapsed while standing and lost consciousness for approximately five minutes. She recovered spontaneously but was extremely weak and dyspneic. She was also diaphoretic and tachypneic, but denied any associated chest pain or palpitations. No tonic-clonic activity was witnessed, and she experienced no incontinence.

Around 1am this morning patient had run of SVT to 150s and became hypotensive. Patient stabilized and sent to Radiology for cross sectional imaging.

Shortly after the patient has returned from imaging, you call the radiologist to find out the preliminary results.

Case Questions:

  1. Name 5 causes of acute chest pain?
  2. What is the primary pathologic injury in acute pulmonary embolism?
  3. What diagnostic imaging options are available for the evaluation of pulmonary embolism?
