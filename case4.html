Case 4

"Unintentional weight loss"

You are rotating through Diagnostic Radiology and reviewing studies with the radiology residents and attending.

The patient is a 78 year old male who you saw in clinic during your rotation on internal medicine. At the time, he was complaining of chronic cough and unintentional weight loss.

Approximately one month ago, patient had a “blind spot” develop in his right eye, in which he could sharply see objects slightly off-center in the visual field of his right eye. Patient then had a visual field cut in the lower portion of his visual field on the left eye.

During an echocardiogram, he was found to have a left atrial mass. He is now admitted to the Cardiac Surgery service for evaluation and treatment of the mass.

PAST MEDICAL AND SURGICAL HISTORY:

  • Hypertension
  • Hyperlipidemia
  • BPH
  • TIA

FAMILY HISTORY:
No history of stroke known in the family

SOCIAL HISTORY:
No history of tobacco use; occasional EtOH; no recreational drug use

Case Questions:

  1. What category of pathology would you characterize the finding?
  2. What is TNM staging and how is imaging used in the determination?
  3. What are most useful imaging modalities in the assessment of lung cancer?
